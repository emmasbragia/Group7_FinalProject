Our Purpose

HASA's goal is to raise awareness and educate Hmong Americans and the community about the Hmong culture and contemporary issues; And to cultivate and foster a familial community where we can all stand together to grow as one.

Our Mission

The mission of the Hmong American Student Association is to:
  • 1. Promote higher education;
  • 2. Serve as a bridge between students, community leaders, and scholars with the Hmong community and the broader American community;
  • 3. Address the concerns and needs of students and the Hmong community such as Hmong Human Rights, Studentsâ€™ Rights and Civil Rights;
  • 4. Provide leadership opportunities for aspiring students;
  • 5. Serve as a resource for students;
  • 6. Serve as a channel to develop, display, and recognize the talents and creativity of the students.

Our History

HASA was first established at the University of Wisconsin-Madison in 198_. With its establishment, the first waves of Hmong individuals to be the first to ever get a college education paved a way for every generation after them to have representation on campus. Ever since then, HASA has transformed in many ways to better suit its time: Promoting Hmong Human Rights, building community, fighting against Asian Hate, and providing cultural shows to display, not only the Hmong culture, but to raise up all underrepresented cultures around us on campus.
